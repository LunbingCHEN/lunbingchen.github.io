Bio-inspired flight, dynamic soaring, and autonomous control in complex flows.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in aeronautics and astronautics at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research focuses on closed-loop dynamic soaring and bio-inspired flight in environmental flows.

My current thesis develops dynamic soaring as a feedback-driven energy-harvesting navigation problem, connecting passive flow mechanisms, biological flight strategies, step-level feedback decision-making, and closed-loop flight implementation.

Research

My work asks how flyers exploit environmental flows for robust and energy-efficient flight. I study this question across passive flow mechanisms, biological flight strategies, feedback decision-making, and autonomous aircraft implementation.

Closed-loop Dynamic Soaring and Energy-harvesting Navigation

Dynamic soaring · optimal control · reinforcement learning · autonomous flight

I investigate dynamic soaring as a closed-loop energy-harvesting navigation problem in environmental shear flows. This work connects optimal-control models, biological flight trajectories, and reinforcement-learning policies for robust target-directed flight.

  • Formulated dynamic soaring as a trade-off between energy harvesting and directional progress.
  • Built 3-DOF gliding-flight models and numerical optimization frameworks for shear-flow flight.
  • Developed deep reinforcement-learning environments for step-level dynamic-soaring navigation.
  • Identified feedback structures that coordinate turning, vertical motion, and energy management.

Passive Flow Mechanisms for Robust Gliding

Flow–structure interaction · passive reconfiguration · turbulence · aerodynamic sensitivity

I study how passive structural and aerodynamic mechanisms improve flight robustness in unsteady and disturbed flows. This work connects canonical experiments with biological wing aerodynamics to understand how flyers reduce load peaks and attenuate disturbance amplification before active control is required.

  • Investigated accelerating reconfigurable circular plates as a model for passive load regulation.
  • Identified load-shifting mechanisms in which flexible structures reduce peak loads and reshape post-peak force histories.
  • Compared avian wings with engineered airfoil wings in turbulent inflow to quantify aerodynamic sensitivity and passive stability.

Unsteady Vortex Dynamics and Force Generation

Vortex rings · impulse theory · accelerating bodies · PIV · low-order force modeling

I investigate vortex-ring growth, impulse-based force generation, and low-order modeling of starting and accelerating flows. This work provides the fluid-mechanics foundation for interpreting transient force histories in separated flows.

  • Conducted force measurements on accelerating plates and related unsteady bluff-body flows.
  • Used time-resolved PIV to characterize vortex-ring formation, vortex growth, and wake evolution.
  • Connected transient force histories with circulation growth, vortex area evolution, and spatial vorticity distribution.

Closed-loop Dynamic Soaring Aircraft Development

UAV platform · state estimation · flight control · system integration

I am developing a closed-loop dynamic-soaring aircraft platform that integrates sensing, decision-making, and execution. The goal is to translate biological and computational dynamic-soaring principles into an autonomous flight system.

  • Designed a perception–decision–execution architecture for wind-relative energy management.
  • Integrated onboard sensing for position, attitude, airspeed, altitude, and wind-relative state estimation.
  • Implemented decision and control modules for step-level maneuver selection, attitude tracking, safety protection, and mode switching.
